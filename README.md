Russian APT Groups – Techniques, Targets, and Strategic Motives 
This research study analyzes the methods, techniques, and strategic motives of the 
Russian Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) teams over their years of cyber espionage and 
sabotage outside their national borders. This article analyzes three notable case studies through 
the lens of cyber intelligence (CYBINT) analysis: the Federal Security Service (FSB) embassy 
espionage operation, the Cold River team's deployment of the LOSTKEYS virus, and the cyber
physical attack on the Norwegian dam. Each instance illustrates the integration of political aims, 
intelligence acquisition, and psychological effects in Russian cyber operations. This research 
shows attribution patterns, motivation, and strategic doctrine that affect Russia's cyber conduct 
by looking at the tactics, methods, and procedures (TTPs) of these APT actors with tools like 
MITRE ATT&CK. The paper ends with suggestions for policies and counterintelligence 
activities to deal with cyber threats from the government. CISA and NSA intelligence advisories, 
Reuters investigative reporting, and cybersecurity research by the Atlantic Council and 
commercial threat intelligence businesses are some of the sources.# russian-apt-analysis
